Flores Island, Portugal
Portugal's Flores Island is known locally as the "cape of flowers" because the area is just teeming with unique and colorful flora. The island also features natural hot springs, lagoons, flood rice fields, cozy villages, lakes, volcanoes and world-famous caves.
Watkins Glen State Park, New York
As one of the most famous of the Finger Lakes State Parks in the United States of America, Watkins Glen State Park will leave you spellbound. Its natural beauty features waterfalls and gorges that you just have to see to believe.
Hinatuan Enchanted River, Philippines
Experience the magic of the Hinatuan Enchanted River in the Philippines. It's a pristine saltwater river that flows into the Pacific Ocean. Locals have long believed the clear 24 meter deep river to be miraculous, and when you see its beauty in person you'll find out why.
Wave Rock, Australia
Just a few hours from Perth stands the 15-meter high Wave Rock which stretches 110 meters through the flat Wheatbelt countryside. It's a unique geological wonder that has been around for three billion years, and one you definitely shouldn't miss.
Zao Fox Village, Japan
The Miyagi Zao mountain isn't just scenic, the area also plays host to the Zao Fox Village. A place where you can hang out with six different species of adorable foxes. Make your trip unforgettable by visiting these wily animals as you take in the breathtaking views.
Ko Adang, Thailand
Still under the radar of most tourists is the beautiful Thai island of Ko Adang. The 30-square kilometer island located within Tarutao National Marine Park has beautiful white sand beaches, stunning coral reefs and lush hills that is just a joy to explore.
